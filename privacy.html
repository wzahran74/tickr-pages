Privacy Policy

Last updated: 10 May 2026

Tickr is a private, offline-first event countdown app. This policy explains exactly what data the app handles. The short version: Tickr does not collect, transmit, or sell any of your data.

What we collect

Nothing. Tickr has no analytics, no telemetry, no advertising SDKs, no crash reporters, and no servers we operate. We have no idea you are using the app, and we cannot read your events.

Where your data lives

  • On your device. Events you create are stored in a local Core Data database on your iPhone, iPad, and Apple Watch.
  • In your private iCloud. If you have iCloud enabled, events sync via Apple's CloudKit private database. This data is owned by you, encrypted by Apple, and inaccessible to us.
  • In your Calendar / Reminders, only when you explicitly tap "Import" — and only for the events you select.
  • In a photo file on your device, only when you choose a photo backdrop for an event.

Permissions Tickr asks for

  • Notifications — to remind you before an event.
  • Calendar — only if you use the import feature.
  • Photo Library — only if you add a photo to an event.

You can revoke any of these in iOS Settings at any time without affecting the rest of the app.

Third parties

Tickr does not include any third-party SDKs that collect data. The only network service used is Apple's CloudKit (only if you enable iCloud sync), which is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

Children

Tickr is rated 4+ and is suitable for all ages. We do not knowingly collect any data — from anyone, of any age.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the "Last updated" date above will change and the new version will be published on this page.
